Cross-section plots

Cross-section plots perform an interpolated, vertical cross section along an arbitrary line. There are two fundamentally different types of cross sections:
  • Zig-zag cross sections plot along a polyline, where each of the vertices is a platform. Normally cross sections of sounding data are done in the zig-zag mode.

  • Planar cross sections plot along a single, arbitrary line, usually drawn with the mouse in a horizontal window. Planar cross sections are usually the method of choice when working with gridded data.
