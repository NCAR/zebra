Adjusting xygraph contour limits

To change the plotting of contours in an xygraph plot, select adjust limits from the left icon menu. The result is a widget like:
Normally, contours are plotted in the autoscale mode, meaning that contour values are assigned in such a way as to fit the data being plotted at the time. There are a number of situations in which you may want to change this behavior, however, including:
  • A glitch in the data causes contours to be spread over a much too wide area;
  • You are generating movies and want to insure that the contour values remain the same over time;
  • You simply do not like the results produced by the autoscale code.
To toggle the scale mode, you need only click on the scale mode button.

In manual mode, you will need to specify center and step values for the contours. The default values in these two blanks are usually the last values produced by the autoscale mode, or the the most recent values you have entered yourself, whichever happened later. They usually make a good starting point for your own values.

Click on apply when you have values you like, or dismiss to get rid of the widget without changing anything.
