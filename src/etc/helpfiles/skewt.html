Skew-t plots

Skew-t plots provide the traditional representation for atmospheric soundings.

Adding data to skew-t plots

Brand-new skew-t plots start with nothing but the grid. Soundings are almost always added to a skew-t plot through the use of active icons in a horizontal plot. Simply select the sounding station of interest, and click on the skew-t window to produce a plot like:

Removing data from skew-t plots

To remove a trace from a skew-t plot, move to the annotation at the top of the skew-t window. The name of each station will illuminate as you move the pointer over it:
When the station you wish to remove is illuminated, simply use the left mouse button to remove the trace from the window.

Other skew-t operations

The plot limits of the skew-t (temperature and pressure bounds) may be changed via the usual adjust limits mechanism.

Some operations are available from the right button menu off the sounding icon:

  • The presence of times in the top annotation may be toggled. If you have disabled times in the top annotation, you may still obtain this information (though in an ugly form, currently), via the overlay times widget.

  • The sounding overlay mode may be toggled. When this mode is on (the default), new soundings will be overlaid on the existing ones (up to a limit of three). When off, a new sounding replaces any existing soundings in the window.

  • The height scale may be toggled between kilometers and feet.
