Planar cross sections

Planar cross sections interpolate data along a single line, usually drawn interactively. While it is possible to do planar cross sections of irregularly-spaced data (i.e. soundings), they are usually only done with gridded data (model output, gridded radar volumes, etc).

Configurations containing a planar cross section almost always contain a horizontal window as well for the control of that cross section. To generate a planar cross section, do the following:

  • Move the pointer into the horizontal window, and to where you would like one end of the cross section plane to be.

  • Push and hold the middle mouse button. The pointer will move slightly, and a line will connect it with its original location.

  • Drag the line until it describes the desired cross section plane.

  • Release the mouse button.
The cross section window will now redraw to reflect the new plane. The control window should have a "window bounds" overlay showing the location of the cross section plane; that, too, will be updated.

If you change your mind about drawing the plane after locating the first endpoint, simply push one of the other mouse buttons while holding down the middle button; the operation will then abort.
