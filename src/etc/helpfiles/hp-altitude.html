Changing the altitude in a horizontal plot

Horizontal plots have an altitude associated with them, which is used to select data (such as three-dimensional grids) which exists at more than one height. Altitudes may be expressed in several different units (such as millibars or km AGL), depending on the data being displayed.

The altitude of a horizontal plot is adjusted through the use of keystrokes in the graphic window. (Note that some window managers may require you to click in the window before keystrokes will be delivered there). The keys relevant to altitude control are:

  • The up and down arrow keys. These keys will move the altitude up or down by one level, with the levels being determined by what is available in the source data.
  • The PgUp and PgDn keys will move to the highest and lowest possible altitude, respectively.
See the list of keys for a more complete description of the keys that are active within graphic windows.
